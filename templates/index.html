About Us

Hateful Meme Detector is developed by students of Vivekanand Education Society's Institute of Technology Under the Guidance of Dr. Sujata Khedkar.

Team Members

  • Priya Karsi
  • Anshul Bahrani
  • Devansh Ahuja

Memes can be an instant attack to people’s emotions because of what they depict in the form of humour, sarcasm. They can attack someone’s nationality, religion, sex, etc leading to a strong negativity in the community around us.

Memes are innocent and funny maximum of the time. Nevertheless, whilst mixed photographs with textual content in an ill-acceptable format, it is able to bring about a hateful meme as a consequence spreading hate withinside the social world.

The venture of effectively figuring out whether or not a meme is hateful or not no longer has come to be surprisingly difficult and similarly important on this enormous international of the internet.

Models

To satisfy the equation of a meme that has image and text as terms, we aim at working with text and image distinctly.

To get the correctness of the model based on both the modalities, the proposed system Combines two image classification models with three different text classification models. In total we experimented with Six different models as follows:

ResNet50 + CNN

ResNet50 + BiLSTM

ResNet50 + Stack LSTM

VGG-16 + CNN

VGG-16 + BiLSTM
